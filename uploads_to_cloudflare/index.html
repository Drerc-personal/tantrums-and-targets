What is This?

The honest truth: Dollar-cost averaging into a low-cost S&P 500 index fund is probably the smartest thing most people can do with their investment dollars.

We know this. And yet.

What if structured discipline and AI collaboration could help us buy quality companies when Mr. Market is having a tantrum? This experiment tests that hypothesis — transparently, with real money, documenting everything.

Three AI analysts debate each decision. One focuses on rules and valuation (The Auditor). One focuses on narrative and macro (The Narrator). One synthesizes and forces a final call (The Arbiter). We reveal it's AI, but not which AI — to avoid tribalism.

The Three Tracks

Track 1: Baseline DCA

$50 into each of 25 stocks, monthly. Limit orders at prior day's close. Never chase.

Track 2: Tantrums

When a stock drops 5%+ in a single day, we pay attention. Is this panic or permanent impairment? The roundtable decides.

Track 3: Targets

Tri-anchor price targets based on historical valuation floors, technical support, and margin-of-safety math. GTC limit orders wait patiently.
